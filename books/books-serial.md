## Simple Reading Order (One Book at a Time)

### **1. Python Crash Course – Eric Matthes**

**Start here. Read fully.**

- Learn Python syntax and basics
    
- Do exercises
    
- This is your foundation
    

---

### **2. Automate the Boring Stuff with Python – Al Sweigart**

**Read selected chapters only**

- Files & folders
    
- Loops and functions
    
- Simple automation
    

Purpose: make Python feel _useful_, not just theoretical.

---

### **3. The Big Book of Small Python Projects – Al Sweigart**

**Practice only**

- Pick 5–10 small projects
    
- Do not aim to finish the book
    

Purpose: build confidence and logic.

---

### **4. The Hundred-Page Machine Learning Book – Andriy Burkov**

**Quick read**

- Read once, no coding
    
- Do not memorize
    

Purpose: understand _what machine learning is_ before coding it.

---

### **5. Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras & TensorFlow – Aurélien Géron**

**Main machine learning book**

- Read slowly
    
- Code everything
    
- This is the most important book in your list
    

---

### **6. Understanding Deep Learning – Simon J. D. Prince**

**After ML basics**

- Read selected chapters
    
- Focus on intuition, not math proofs
    

Purpose: understand neural networks properly.

---

### **7. Why Machines Learn – Anil Ananthaswamy**

**Light reading**

- Read anytime after Book 6
    
- No coding required
    

Purpose: mental clarity and intuition.

---

### **8. Practical MLOps – Noah Gift**

**Engineering mindset**

- Learn how models are used in real systems
    
- No need to read every chapter
    

---

### **9. AI Engineering – Chip Huyen**

**Modern ML systems**

- Complements MLOps
    
- Focus on deployment and scaling
    

---

### **10. Hands-On Large Language Models – Alammar & Grootendorst**

**Final book**

- Only after completing ML + DL basics
    
- Hands-on, practical
    

---

### **11. The Elements of Statistical Learning**

**Reference only (optional)**

- Do NOT read from start to end
    
- Use only when needed for theory
    

---
